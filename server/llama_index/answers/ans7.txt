COMPREHENSIVE ANSWERS FOR MSC 5-YEAR PROGRAMME (R2024 REGULATIONS)
All answers derived from R2024-MSC-Regulations.pdf via RAG tool

Questions 1-15: Programme Duration & Credits
1. If I joined this programme in 2024, which regulation applies to me?
R2024 MSc Regulations apply to students admitted from 2024-2025 onward.

2. Will these regulations change if I take a break of study?
Yes. After rejoining from break of study, you are governed by the rules and regulations in force at the time of rejoining.

3. What is the normal duration of this programme?
10 consecutive semesters (5 years).

4. What is the maximum time allowed to complete the programme?
16 semesters (8 years).

5. Is break of study counted within the maximum duration?
Yes. The total duration includes the period of break of study.

6. What happens if I exceed the maximum duration?
You become ineligible for the award of the degree.

7. Can I request extension beyond the maximum duration?
No. Extension beyond the prescribed period shall not be permitted.

8. How many total credits are required to complete this programme?
The specific total credit requirement is defined in the curriculum (not explicitly stated in retrieved sections).

9. Can I graduate if I am short of one or two credits?
No. You must successfully gain the required number of total credits specified in the curriculum.

10. Are all courses in the curriculum mandatory?
The curriculum includes various categories of courses. Course categorization and mandates are specified in the curriculum.

11. Are there electives in this programme?
Yes. Elective courses are part of the curriculum.

12. How many elective credits are required?
Elective credit requirements are specified in the curriculum (not explicitly stated in retrieved sections).

13. Can I choose electives from different areas?
Yes. If attendance is short in an elective, you may register for the same or any other elective course.

14. Can I skip one elective category and compensate with another?
Not explicitly addressed in retrieved sections. Refer to curriculum specifications.

15. Is there a minimum credit requirement per semester?
Not explicitly stated in retrieved sections.

Questions 16-30: Add/Drop & Online Courses
16. Can I overload credits in a semester to finish early?
You can earn more than the prescribed credits, but maximum credit enrollment per semester is capped.

17. Is there a maximum credit limit per semester?
Yes. Maximum 30 credits per semester.

18. What happens if I register for fewer credits in a semester?
Not explicitly addressed, but you must meet curriculum requirements for degree eligibility.

19. Is add/drop option available?
Yes. Add/Drop is available.

20. When can I apply for add/drop?
Within two weeks from the start of the semester (from first to ninth semesters).

21. Is add/drop compulsory or optional?
Optional. It's an option given to candidates.

22. Is there a limit on add/drop credits during the programme?
Yes. Total cannot exceed 8 credits during the entire programme.

23. Are online courses allowed?
Yes.

24. Which platforms are approved for online courses?
NPTEL, SWAYAM, Universities, and Other Agencies approved by Board of Studies.

25. Do online course credits count towards degree completion?
Yes. Credits earned may be transferred.

26. Do online course grades affect GPA or CGPA?
Yes. If grades are assigned, they are included in GPA/CGPA calculations.

27. Are self-study courses allowed?
Yes.

28. How many self-study courses can I take?
One per semester.

29. Are self-study courses evaluated like regular courses?
Yes. Same assessment mode as other courses.

30. Are audit courses available in this programme?
Yes.

Questions 31-45: Audit Courses & Attendance
31. Do audit courses affect GPA or CGPA?
No. Audit courses have no grade points assigned and are not counted for GPA/CGPA.

32. What grade is given for audit courses?
SC (Successfully Completed) if performance grade is obtained.

33. What happens if I fail an audit course?
If you don't obtain a performance grade, the course won't be listed on your grade sheet.

34. How is attendance calculated – semester-wise or course-wise?
Both. Overall semester attendance AND per-course attendance must be satisfied.

35. What is the minimum overall attendance required?
Not less than 80% (after rounding) of overall attendance in a semester.

36. What is the minimum attendance required in each course?
Not less than 60% in each course.

37. If my overall attendance is fine but one subject attendance is low, what happens?
You cannot appear for that specific course's end semester examination. You must repeat that course when offered next.

38. Can I write exams for other subjects if one subject attendance is insufficient?
Yes, if attendance requirements are met for those other subjects.

39. Is medical attendance condonation available?
Yes. With hospitalization/accident/specific illness and medical certificate.

40. What is the minimum attendance required for medical condonation?
Not less than 70% in the current semester.

41. How many times can medical condonation be used?
Only twice during the entire duration of the programme.

42. Is startup attendance concession allowed?
Yes. For entrepreneurship/startup activities.

43. What is the minimum attendance required for startup concession?
Not less than 60% in the current semester.

44. Who approves startup attendance concession?
Review committee recommendation + Principal's approval.

45. Do lab courses have separate attendance requirements?
Yes. Each course (including practical/lab) has the 60% minimum attendance requirement.

Questions 46-60: Exam Registration & Evaluation
46. What happens if I lack lab attendance but meet theory attendance?
Attendance is calculated per course. If lab attendance is below 60%, you cannot appear for that lab course examination.

47. Is exam registration mandatory?
Yes. Registration is mandatory for current semester and arrear courses.

48. What happens if I do not register for exams?
You will not be permitted to move on to the higher semester.

49. Is separate registration needed for arrear courses?
Yes. Registration is mandatory for arrear courses as well.

50. Can I move to the next semester without exam registration?
No.

51. How are theory courses evaluated?
CA 40 marks (average of best two tests out of three, plus tutorial/others, plus assignment) + ESE 60 marks (conducted for 100, reduced to 60). Total rounded to nearest integer. Note: Document shows CA 50 + ESE 50 in one section, with tests reduced to 30 marks each for CA.

52. How many marks are for continuous assessment?
40 marks (or 50 marks based on specific clause - verify with programme curriculum).

53. How many marks are for end semester examination?
60 marks (or 50 marks based on specific clause - verify with programme curriculum).

54. Is passing the end semester exam mandatory?
Yes. Minimum 45% of ESE marks is required.

55. What is the minimum mark required to pass a course?
Not less than 50% total (CA + ESE) with minimum 45% in ESE.

56. Are internal marks carried forward for arrear attempts?
Yes. CA marks from first attempt are retained for subsequent attempts through the third attempt.

57. Can I improve my grade after passing a course?
No.

58. Is grade improvement allowed in this programme?
No. Once passed, reappearance for improvement is not permitted.

59. Is withdrawal from examination allowed?
Yes, for valid reasons.

60. How many times can I withdraw during the programme?
Only once during the entire duration.

Questions 61-75: Withdrawal, Break & GPA/CGPA
61. Can I withdraw from final semester examinations?
Yes. Final semester candidates who withdraw from project viva-voce can appear for supplementary viva-voce.

62. Does withdrawal count as an appearance?
No. Withdrawal is not considered an appearance for classification purposes.

63. What happens if I am absent for an exam?
Absence after registration is considered an appearance for classification purposes.

64. Is absence treated as a failed attempt?
Yes, absence counts as an attempt.

65. Does absence affect degree classification?
Yes. Absence after registration is treated as an appearance for classification.

66. Is break of study permitted?
Yes, for valid reasons (accident, hospitalization, prolonged ill health, etc.).

67. How many times can break of study be taken?
Once for one year maximum. In extraordinary situations, additional break not exceeding one more year may be granted.

68. What is the maximum duration of break of study?
One year normally; up to two years total in extraordinary situations.

69. Which regulation applies after rejoining from break?
Regulations in force at the time of rejoining.

70. Can additional courses be prescribed after rejoining?
Yes. Additional/equivalent courses may be prescribed to bridge curriculum differences.

71. What happens if I do not report for two consecutive semesters?
Your name shall be deleted permanently from college enrollment.

72. Can my admission be cancelled permanently?
Yes. After two consecutive semesters of non-reporting, permanent deletion occurs with no readmission rights.

73. How is GPA calculated?
GPA = (Sum of course credits × grade points for all courses in the semester) / (Sum of course credits for all courses in that semester)

74. How is CGPA calculated?
CGPA = (Sum of course credits × grade points for all courses from first to final semester) / (Sum of course credits for all courses from first to final semester)

75. Is GPA or CGPA calculated if I have arrears?
No. GPA and CGPA are computed only for candidates with a pass in all courses.

Questions 76-90: Classification, Revaluation & Degree Award
76. How is CGPA converted into percentage?
Not explicitly mentioned in the retrieved sections.

77. What CGPA is required for First Class?
Not less than 6.50 (must pass all courses within 12 consecutive semesters excluding authorized break).

78. What CGPA is required for First Class with Distinction?
Not less than 8.50 (for regular students passing all courses in first appearance within 10 semesters).
Not less than 9.00 (for transfer/readmitted students).

79. Do arrears affect degree classification?
Yes. Distinction requires passing all courses in first appearance.

80. Does withdrawal affect classification?
No. Withdrawal is not considered an appearance for classification.

81. Can grades be withheld?
Yes.

82. Under what conditions can grades be withheld?
If dues are not cleared, or if disciplinary case is pending, or for any other reason.

83. What happens if a disciplinary case is pending?
Grades may be withheld and you won't be eligible for degree award.

84. Can results be changed after publication?
Results can be changed through revaluation if applied for and approved.

85. Is revaluation allowed?
Yes, for Theory courses and Theory cum Practical courses where ESE is involved. Must first obtain photocopy, get recommendation from faculty and course coordinator.

86. Is photocopy of answer script allowed?
Yes, on payment of prescribed fee.

87. Are supplementary exams conducted?
Yes. For Project Work I/II failures (if ESE marks < 50%), candidate must resubmit report within 30 days and appear for supplementary viva-voce.

88. What are the final eligibility conditions for award of the degree?

Successfully completed all courses under different categories
Gained required total credits within stipulated time
Passed any additional courses prescribed after readmission
No disciplinary action pending
89. Who has the authority to amend these regulations?
Academic Council (through the institution).

90. When is the degree officially awarded?
After satisfying all eligibility conditions. Specific timing not mentioned in retrieved sections.
