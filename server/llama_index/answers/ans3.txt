ANSWERS FOR Q3.TXT - MCA SPECIFIC QUESTIONS

Questions 1-15 Response:

R2024-MCA Regulations apply if you joined MCA in 2024.

Yes, regulation can change if you take break of study - you'll be governed by regulations in force at the time of rejoining.

Duration: MCA should be completed in 2 years (4 semesters) normally, but not more than 4 years (8 semesters).

Extension not permitted beyond 8 semesters.

Minimum 84 credits required to complete MCA programme.

6-9. Bridge courses:

Yes, compulsory for non-CS/IT/CA background students
Four bridge courses: Problem Solving Techniques, C++ Programming, Computer Organization, Discrete Mathematics
No credits assigned - they are non-credit courses
Evaluated through 2 continuous assessment tests (50 marks each)
NOT included in CGPA calculation
10-12. Electives: Number specified in curriculum. Can choose from Professional Electives (PE). Exemptions possible if you earn required credits through one/two credit courses, online courses, or self-study courses in advance (up to third semester).

13-15. Credit limits:

Maximum 30 credits per semester
Maximum 15 credits total through one/two credit, online, and self-study courses
Maximum 6 credits through Add/Drop courses during entire programme
Must earn total credits specified in curriculum
Questions 16-30 Response:

16-18. Attendance: BOTH semester-wise AND subject-wise:

80% overall attendance required
60% minimum per course required
19-20. Medical condonation:

Yes, if you have 70%+ attendance, can apply with medical certificate and condonation fee
Can avail twice during entire MCA programme
21-24. Startup concession:

Not explicitly mentioned in MCA regulations provided
Other programmes show startup concession - check with institution
25-26. Lab attendance: Yes, 60% required in each course. Lacking lab attendance prevents writing that course's exam specifically.

27-30. Mini Project:

MCA has Mini Project as part of curriculum
Evaluated: 50% continuous assessment + 50% end semester (viva-voce)
Semester offerings specified in curriculum
Failure: Must redo in subsequent semester when offered
Questions 31-45 Response:

Yes, can be repeated if failed - must reenroll and satisfy attendance requirements.
32-33. Internship:

Projects through Internships option available
Submit report and certificate from organization
Evaluated: continuous assessment (50%) + viva-voce (50%)
Yes, project marks included in CGPA.
35-37. Project submissions:

Non-submission by specified date = deemed failed, must redo
<50% in internal (CA) = cannot submit report, must redo with attendance
Fail viva (<50% ESE) = resubmit report within 30 days, fresh viva-voce
Publication NOT mandatory for MCA Mini Project (unlike ME/MTech where it's required for major project).
39-41. Online courses:

Yes, allowed from NPTEL/SWAYAM/approved agencies
Maximum 15 credits through all one/two credit, online, and self-study courses combined
Yes, affect CGPA - credits transferred and grades assigned
42-45. Audit courses:

Optional courses not in your curriculum, taken without grade pressure
Maximum one per semester, two total during programme
NO effect on GPA/CGPA
Grade: SC (Successfully Completed) if performance grade achieved
Questions 46-60 Response:

46-48. Passing requirements:

50% total (CA + ESE combined)
Minimum 45% in ESE - YES, ESE passing mandatory
Yes, internal marks carried forward from first attempt (valid until 4th attempt)
49-52. Withdrawal:

Yes, allowed once during entire programme
Must apply before last examination of semester
Does NOT count as attempt for classification
Cannot withdraw in final semester
53-54. Absence:

Marked as AB (Absent), treated as fail
Counted for classification - considered as appeared attempt
55-56. Grade improvement: NOT allowed - once passed, cannot reappear for improvement.

57-60. GPA/CGPA:

GPA = Σ(credit×grade points in semester) ÷ Σ(credits in semester)
CGPA = Σ(credit×grade points all semesters) ÷ Σ(credits all semesters)
Only computed when all courses passed
CGPA to percentage conversion not specified in provided context
Questions 61-75 Response:

61-62. Classification (based on MSc pattern shown - MCA likely similar):

First Class: CGPA ≥ 6.50 (complete within 12 semesters)
Distinction: CGPA ≥ 8.50 (all courses first appearance within consecutive semesters)
63-64. Arrears and withdrawal:

Yes, arrears disqualify for Distinction (requires all first-time passes)
Withdrawal does NOT count as appearance for classification
65-67. Break of study:

Yes, allowed for valid reasons
Once normally for max 1 year; extraordinary: additional 1 year with fee
Yes, counted in maximum duration (still cannot exceed 8 semesters total)
68-69. After rejoining:

Regulations in force at rejoining time apply
Yes, additional/equivalent courses may be prescribed to bridge old and new curriculum
70-71. Non-reporting:

Not reporting for two consecutive semesters without intimation
Name deleted permanently - no readmission allowed
72-74. Grade withholding:

Yes, can be withheld if unsettled dues, pending disciplinary case, or other valid reasons
Disciplinary action pending prevents degree award
Results generally cannot be changed after declaration without proper revaluation process.
Questions 76-90 Response:

Yes, photocopy of answer script - apply with fee, faculty reviews, then apply for revaluation

88-90. Degree award:

Degree awarded after satisfying all eligibility conditions
