ANSWERS TO MSC 5-YEAR PROGRAMME QUESTIONS (Q5.TXT)
Questions 1-15: Programme Basics & Attendance
1. Which regulation applies if I joined MSc 5-year in 2024?
R2024 MSc Regulations apply for students joining in 2024.

2. What is the total duration of the programme?
10 consecutive semesters (5 Years).

3. What is the maximum duration allowed?
16 semesters (8 Years).

4. What happens if I exceed maximum duration?
Extension beyond 16 semesters is not permitted. You become ineligible for degree award.

5. How many total credits are required to graduate?
The document states minimum credits are required but doesn't specify the exact number in the retrieved sections. Refer to the programme curriculum for specific credit requirements.

6. Is attendance calculated semester-wise or course-wise?
Both - overall semester attendance AND per-course attendance must be satisfied.

7. What is minimum attendance required overall?
Not less than 80% (after rounding off to nearest integer) of overall attendance in a semester.

8. What is minimum attendance per course?
Not less than 60% attendance in each course.

9. Can medical reasons reduce attendance?
Yes. If you secure not less than 70% attendance due to medical reasons (hospitalization/accident/specific illness), you may appear for exams with Principal's approval on payment of condonation fee.

10. How many times can medical condonation be used?
Only twice during the entire duration of the degree programme.

11. Is startup attendance concession allowed?
Yes. Students in entrepreneurship/startup activities can get concession.

12. What is minimum attendance for startup students?
Not less than 60% in the current semester.

13. Who approves startup attendance?
Review committee recommendation + Principal's approval.

14. What happens if one course attendance is low?
If you don't meet the 60% requirement in a specific course, you cannot appear for that course's end semester examination. You must repeat that course when offered next.

15. Do lab courses have separate attendance?
Yes, practical courses have separate attendance tracked independently.

Questions 16-30: Employability Courses & Projects
16. Can I pass theory if lab attendance is short?
No. You must satisfy attendance requirements for each course independently. If practical attendance is short, you cannot appear for that component's examination.

17. What are employability enhancement courses?
Courses like project work, internship, professional skills training/industrial training, and entrepreneurships/start ups to gain/exhibit knowledge/skills.

18. Are professional skills training courses mandatory?
Yes, it's part of the programme structure.

19. How many credits do skill training courses carry?
Two credits for Phase I (fifth semester) and two credits for Phase II (sixth semester), totaling 4 credits.

20. Can startup replace skill training?
Yes. Student entrepreneurs may earn maximum 2 credits per semester for two semesters in place of Professional Skills Training I/II.

21. How are startups evaluated?
Through periodic reviews by a panel of members constituted by the Principal.

22. Is internship mandatory?
Not mandatory but provided as an option for full-time internship during entire seventh semester and/or final semester.

23. Can internship replace project?
Yes. Internship credits can replace Project Work I in seventh semester and/or Project Work II in final semester.

24. How many credits does internship carry?
Same number of credits as corresponding Project Work (Project Work I or Project Work II).

25. What happens if internship certificate is missing?
The retrieved sections don't explicitly address this. Consult with college administration.

26. What is Mini Project?
A project component mentioned in the programme structure. Specific details not provided in retrieved sections.

27. What happens if I fail Mini Project?
Mini Project follows continuous assessment re-enrollment rules - you must re-enroll in subsequent semester and satisfy attendance requirements.

28. What is Project Work I and II?
Project Work I (seventh semester) and Project Work II (final semester) - major project components evaluated through 50 CA marks + 50 ESE marks.

29. Are project marks included in CGPA?
Yes, project marks are included in CGPA calculation.

30. Is publication mandatory for MSc project?
The retrieved sections do not explicitly state publication requirements for MSc projects.

Questions 31-45: Project Details, Audit Courses & Exams
31. What happens if project report is late?
If you fail to secure 50% ESE marks in Project Work I/II, you must resubmit the Project Report within 30 days from result declaration date.

32. Can project be repeated?
Yes. If you fail project, you can register for supplementary examination and viva voce will be conducted.

33. What are audit courses?
Courses not listed in your programme curriculum that you can register for without getting a 'good' grade, only for learning purposes.

34. How many audit courses can I take?
One audit course per semester starting from second semester, maximum two courses during entire programme.

35. Do audit courses affect CGPA?
No. Since audit courses have no grade points assigned, they're not counted for GPA and CGPA calculations.

36. What grade is given for audit course?
SC (Successfully Completed) - if you obtain performance grade. Performance grade itself is not shown.

37. What are mandatory courses?
Student Induction Program including Universal Human Values and Yoga, etc. at the beginning of first semester.

38. Do mandatory courses affect CGPA?
No. No credits given and grade "SC" (Successfully Completed) is assigned. Not counted for GPA/CGPA calculations.

39. What is minimum mark to pass a course?
Not less than 50% of total marks (CA + ESE combined) with minimum 45% in ESE for courses with ESE. For continuous assessment only courses: minimum 50% in CA marks.

40. Is ESE passing mandatory?
Yes. Minimum 45% of marks prescribed for end semester examination is mandatory.

41. Are internal marks retained for arrears?
Yes. CA marks secured in first attempt are retained and considered valid for subsequent attempts (up to third attempt).

42. From which attempt grade is capped?
From fourth attempt onwards, only ESE marks are considered (CA is not counted), and you must secure minimum 50% in ESE. Grade awarded will be only the lowest passing grade irrespective of marks secured.

43. Is revaluation allowed?
Yes. Apply for photocopy first, then pursue with faculty member and course coordinator. Based on recommendation, apply for revaluation to Controller of Examinations.

44. Is photocopy of answer script allowed?
Yes. You can apply for photocopy of semester examination answer script on payment of prescribed fee.

45. Can I withdraw from exams?
Yes, for valid reasons.

Questions 46-60: Withdrawal, GPA/CGPA & Classification
46. How many times can withdrawal be used?
Only once during the entire duration of the degree programme.

47. Can I withdraw final semester project viva?
Yes. Final semester candidates who withdraw from project viva-voce for genuine reasons can appear for supplementary viva-voce examination within reasonable time.

48. Does withdrawal affect classification?
No. Withdrawal is not considered as an appearance for deciding eligibility for First Class with Distinction/First Class.

49. Is absence treated as attempt?
Yes. Absence after registering for ESE is considered an appearance for classification purposes.

50. Can I improve grade after passing?
No. A candidate who has passed a course is not entitled to reappear for improvement of letter grades/marks.

51. How many times grade improvement allowed?
Not allowed. Once you pass, no improvement attempts permitted.

52. How is GPA calculated?
GPA = (Sum of course credits × grade points for all courses in the semester) / (Sum of course credits for all courses in that semester)

53. How is CGPA calculated?
CGPA = (Sum of course credits × grade points for all courses from first to final semester) / (Sum of course credits for all courses from first to final semester)

54. Is CGPA calculated if arrears exist?
No. GPA and CGPA are computed only for candidates with a pass in all courses.

55. How is CGPA converted to percentage?
Not explicitly mentioned in retrieved sections.

56. What CGPA is required for First Class?
Not less than 6.50 CGPA (must pass all courses within 12 consecutive semesters excluding authorized break).

57. What CGPA is required for Distinction?
Not less than 8.50 CGPA (if passed all courses in first appearance within 10 semesters). For transfer/readmitted students: not less than 9.00 CGPA.

58. Do arrears affect distinction?
Yes. Must pass all courses in first appearance within 10 consecutive semesters for distinction eligibility.

59. Does withdrawal affect distinction?
No. Withdrawal is not considered as an appearance for distinction eligibility.

60. Can I take break of study?
Yes, for valid reasons (accident, hospitalization, prolonged ill health, etc.).

Questions 61-75: Break of Study, Degree Eligibility & Supplementary
61. How many times break allowed?
Once during entire period of study for maximum one year. In extraordinary situations, additional break not exceeding another one year may be granted on payment of prescribed fee.

62. Is break counted in max duration?
Yes. Total programme completion time including break cannot exceed 16 semesters (8 years).

63. Which regulation applies after rejoining?
Rules and regulations in force at the time of rejoining.

64. Can extra courses be imposed after rejoining?
Yes. If rejoining under new regulations, you must apply for prescribing additional/equivalent courses to bridge curriculum differences.

65. What happens if I don't report for two semesters?
Your name will be deleted permanently from college enrollment.

66. Can my name be removed permanently?
Yes. If you don't report for two consecutive semesters without intimation, permanent removal occurs. No readmission allowed under any circumstances.

67. Can grades be withheld?
Yes.

68. Under what conditions grades are withheld?

If you haven't cleared dues
If disciplinary case is pending against you
For any other reason as determined by authorities
69. What happens if disciplinary case exists?
Grades may be withheld. You won't be eligible for degree award if disciplinary action is pending.

70. Can results be changed later?
Results can be changed through revaluation process if applied for and approved.

71. Are supplementary exams allowed?
Yes. For Project Work I/II failures (less than 50% ESE marks), supplementary viva-voce examination is conducted. Also for sports participants during ESE period.

72. Can I write arrears with regular exams?
Yes. Registration is mandatory for both current semester and arrear courses.

73. Can I graduate with arrears?
No. Must successfully pass all courses required for the programme.

74. What are final eligibility conditions for degree?

Successfully completed all courses under different categories
Gained required total credits within stipulated time
Passed any additional courses prescribed after readmission
No disciplinary action pending
75. Who approves regulations?
Academic Council of Kongu Engineering College.
