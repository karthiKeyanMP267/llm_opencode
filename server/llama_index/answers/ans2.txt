ANSWERS FOR Q2.TXT - BSc SPECIFIC QUESTIONS

Questions 1-15 Response:

R2024-BSc Regulations apply if you joined BSc in 2024.

Maximum duration: BSc degree must be completed in 3 years (6 semesters) normally, but not more than 5 years (10 semesters).

3-4. Exceeding maximum duration: Extension beyond 5 years shall not be permitted. You cannot continue after max duration.

5-6. Credits required: Total credits specified in curriculum must be earned. Cannot graduate with credit shortage - all required credits mandatory.

7-8. Attendance calculation: BOTH semester-wise AND subject-wise:

80% overall attendance required (semester-wise)
60% minimum per course required (subject-wise)
If overall is fine but one subject is low (<60%), you cannot write that course's exam
9-11. Minimum attendance:

Standard: 80% overall
Medical condonation: 70% overall (can avail only once during entire BSc programme)
Must also maintain 60% in each course
Startup concession for BSc: Yes, applicable. Need 60% overall attendance with review committee recommendation and Principal approval.

62% attendance: If for startup reasons and you have 60%+ in each course, you may be eligible with approval. Otherwise, you are detained.

14-15. Practical/lab attendance: Yes, 60% attendance required separately in each course including labs. Missing lab attendance prevents you from appearing for that specific course exam.

Questions 16-30 Response:

16-17. Passing requirements:

50% total marks (CA + ESE combined)
Minimum 45% in ESE - YES, ESE passing is mandatory
18-19. Internal marks for arrears: YES, carried forward. Continuous assessment marks from first attempt are retained and valid for subsequent attempts (until 4th attempt when only ESE marks count).

20-22. Grade improvement: NOT allowed. Once you pass, you cannot reappear for improvement of grades/marks.

23-25. Withdrawal:

Yes, allowed once during entire programme for valid reasons
Must apply before last examination of semester
Does NOT count as attempt for classification purposes
26-27. Absence: Marked as AB (Absent), treated as fail, and counts as an attempt.

28-30. Break of study:

Yes, allowed for valid reasons (hospitalization, medical emergencies)
Maximum one year normally, can extend to two years in extraordinary situations
YES, counted in maximum duration (total still cannot exceed 10 semesters)
Questions 31-45 Response:

Rejoin with different batch: Yes, after break of study, you rejoin and are governed by regulations in force at time of rejoining.
32-34. Credit limits per semester:

Maximum 30 credits per semester
Additional courses limited to 8 credits total during entire programme (Add/Drop option)
Minimum credit requirements must be met to progress
35-36. Online courses:

Yes, allowed from NPTEL/SWAYAM/approved agencies
Credits transferred and grades assigned accordingly
Maximum 24 credits through all value-added, online, and self-study courses combined
37-39. Audit courses:

NO effect on GPA/CGPA - no grade points assigned
Grade given: SC (Successfully Completed) if performance grade obtained
Failing audit course: Does not matter - won't be listed in grade sheet
40-45. GPA/CGPA and Classification:

GPA = Σ(credit×grade points in semester) ÷ Σ(credits in semester)
CGPA = Σ(credit×grade points all semesters) ÷ Σ(credits all semesters)
CGPA calculated from first semester through final semester
GPA/CGPA only computed for candidates who passed all courses
First Class with Distinction requires passing all courses in first appearance within consecutive semesters
Number of attempts DOES affect classification - multiple attempts disqualify from Distinction
Questions 46-60 Response:

Arrears for graduation: No - must successfully complete all courses and earn required credits to graduate.
47-48. Grades withheld: Yes, grades may be withheld if:

Unsettled dues
Pending disciplinary case
Any other valid reason
49-50. Result approval: Controller of Examinations handles examination processes. Results generally cannot be changed after grade sheet issued without proper revaluation.

Malpractice: Liable for punitive action as per examination rules prescribed by college.
52-53. Practical marks: Yes, included in CGPA. Each course (theory, practical, theory-cum-practical) contributes to CGPA. Must pass each course component independently.

54-58. Project evaluation:

Evaluated via continuous assessment (50%) + viva-voce (50%)
Report submission mandatory by specified date
Yes, project compulsory for degree completion
Non-submission = deemed failed, must redo in subsequent semester
Can be repeated if failed
BSc offers internship option in final semester in place of Project Work II
59-60. Skill enhancement courses: Programme-specific; evaluated and graded but may not carry credits or affect CGPA depending on course type.

Questions 61-75 Response:

Skill courses affect CGPA: Depends on course type - some carry credits and affect CGPA, others don't.

Curriculum approval: Board of Studies and Academic Council approve curriculum.

63-64. Regulations mid-course:

Academic Council can modify/amend regulations without notice
After break of study, regulations in force at rejoining time apply
65-66. Rule conflicts and relaxation: Academic Council of Kongu Engineering College has final authority. Department must follow institutional regulations.

67-68. Revaluation:

YES, allowed for Theory and Theory-cum-Practical courses with ESE
Process: First apply for photocopy of answer script, faculty reviews and recommends, then apply for revaluation
NOT permitted for pure practical courses
69-70. Supplementary exams and attempts:

Supplementary exams conducted for project viva failures
No specific limit on attempts, but must complete within maximum duration (10 semesters)
From 4th attempt onwards, only ESE marks considered (must score 50% in ESE)
71-72. Arrears with regular exams:

YES, registration mandatory for both current semester and arrear courses
Arrears disqualify for First Class with Distinction (requires all first-time passes)
73-75. Appeals and certificates: Revaluation serves as appeal mechanism. Provisional and degree certificates issued per institutional procedures.

Questions 76-85 Response:

Degree certificate: Issued after satisfying all requirements in clause related to degree eligibility.

Degree cancellation: Subject to disciplinary action, malpractice findings, or other violations.

78-80. Unknowing violations:

Regulations are binding on all students
College reserves right to modify regulations without notice
Students responsible for knowing applicable regulations
Final authority: Academic Council of Kongu Engineering College has authority to modify/amend regulations, curricula, syllabi, examination schemes, procedures, requirements, and rules.
